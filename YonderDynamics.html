Yonder Dynamics Competition Videos

Overview

Project Type

These were video projects for a student robotics organization, Yonder Dynamics.

Objectives

To help Yonder Dynamics get accepted into an annual world-wide rover competition, the University Rover Challenge, at Utah and to help fundraise their efforts into bulding their rover.

Duration

Over a month to get footage, less than a week to edit each videos with varying times devoted to each video.

My Role

As a videographer for Yonder Dynamics, I collaborated with Yonder Dynamics to regularly get quality footage of student teams building aspects of the rover. I either collaborated with a couple of members on the video editing process or I independently made the videos.

Tools

Premiere Pro, Photoshop

Affiliations

Yonder Dynamics

Background

Yonder Dynamics prepares all year long, and more, to have all student members assemble a Mars rover for the chance to have their rover compete in the annual rover competition against other universities around the world called the University Rover Challenge. Across multiple attempts in making these videos, I have been tasked to help project managers and business leads cover video submissions for their Systems Acceptance Review submission (as part of one of the deliverables for the University Rover Challenge) as well as fundraising videos to help support their efforts in constructing the Mars rover.

To learn more about the student organization, check out Yonder Dynamics' website.

First Video - Fundraising Video

Introduction to Project

Throughout the process of making the first video, I was mostly involved in gathering footage of people during days where executive student members and/or student teams were available to work on the rover. During in-person meetings and online discussions with people, I listened to their expectations that they had with the media content they wanted to capture as well as their updates to the progress of other teams within Yonder regarding set up of the rover.

At the time, I was a recently recruited member of Yonder Dynamics as an ad-hoc videographer. I worked indirectly with a partner, Joung Hun Suk, to help capture new footage, cover a voiceover, and utilize past footage that Yonder Dynamics provided to edit the video with the newly captured footage. The video would eventually be uploaded into Yonder Dynamics' Kickstarter page.

Capturing New Footage

I filmed two executive members of Yonder Dynamics who volunteered themselves to be on camera to express what they have planned for the script that they helped made. Kirk Hutchison was the student director for Yonder dynamics at the time that gave the introduction of what Yonder Dynamics was. The other person was Allison Kubo, who was one of the project managers who talked about the importance the Kickstarter meant for Yonder’s progress towards the competition.

I also captured more footage of the rover moving around and general B-Roll footage of student team members collaborating and working in a room environment.

Using a New Voiceover and Past Footage

The majority of the video consisted of a single person talking about the different developments and physical parts (chassis, metal arm, wheels, etc.) of the potential rover, which would be voiced by another former member of Yonder Dynamics, Victoria McKee. With her part of Yonder Dynamics' script established, I did a voiceover session with her to ensure that the video had quality audio from her when she needed to articulate these different sections of the video about the rover.


In tandem with her voiceover, the footage provided would mostly consist of Yonder Dynamics’ footage of the actual rover during their prior competition as well as animations of Computer-Aided Design (CAD) renderings of rover parts made in SolidWorks by other student members of Yonder Dynamics.

Post-Production

As for post-production, I handed off the latest draft to Joung Hun Suk, a former videographer for Yonder Dynamics, so that he could assemble the rest of the footage into the video and create the finishing touches to Yonder Dynamics' branding.

Second Video - 2018 Systems Acceptance Review

Introduction to Project

I was requested to return to Yonder Dynamics to help out with their video submission as part of one of their deliverables for the Systems Acceptance Review. The Systems Acceptance Review (SAR) is for university teams to submit as a requirement to have judges evaluate their work and determine which teams are qualified enough to compete in the University Rover Challenge. Along with a giant report to submit for SAR, a video is required to supplement the report and to prove visually that the rover is at a point where it would be ready for the competition. Much more was demanded from the rules of the 2018 SAR as the video had to be within 5 minutes and it had to show the rover’s parts, what the rover’s capabilities are, and how the rover would be ready for the challenges faced in the competition.

I worked with Joung Hun Suk, who helped with the 2017 Fundraising video, as well as a student member of the science subteam, JJ, to work on video logistics. The three of us worked together with the business lead (Isabelle Yan), lead mechanical engineer (Kyle Gillespie), and other executive members to coordinate available days and times for the three ofus to frequently capture media content of other students in Yonder working on the rover or work on the voiceovers.

Footage Captured - Rover Physical Parts

These are clips that showcase the process of making and refining each physical part of the rover.

Footage Captured - Rover Features

These are additional functionalites that the rover would achieve from other parts installed that will show that the rover is capable of accomplishing challenges set forth by the Unviersity Rover Challenge, such as environment mapping and soil analysis.

Footage Captured - CAD Animations of Rover Parts

Computer-Aided Design renderings and animations of different rover parts to further illustrate the composition and movement of the rover parts. All of the credit belongs to the various students in Yonder who helped to gather high-quality renderings of the CAD models and generate the animations.

Footage Captured - Outside Environment

I personally helped film the rover during one afternoon, and the majority of the clips occurred either near the lab that Yonder Dynamics worked on their rover or they were farther away into more natural terrain. Recording the rover in these environments produced footage that can demonstrate the rover's capabilities in traversing over rough terrain and overcoming obstacles along its way.

Post-Production and Soundtracks

I helped to coordinate editing tasks with Joung and JJ through breaking up the labor of editing the video into smaller manageable portions so that each person would be able to independently edit and complete a portion of the video, thus making the post-production process more efficient. The majority of the soundtracks that the three of us worked with had a mix of professional, corporate tones and energetic tones. The soundtracks placed were based on the intended tone for each part of the video. More corporate-like soundtracks were placed in the beginning and middle of the video while more energetic sountracks were placed towards the end.


Visual Graphics and Effects

I helped to include Yonder Dynamics' logo, title text "URC Systems Acceptance Review 2018", and other relevant text. I 6applied fade-in and fade-out combined with a blur to smoothly make the graphics display and disappear from the beginning and end of the video. Since the video mostly comprised of sections representing each student subteam on Yonder Dynamics (which includes motions, drive system, science, electrical, and software) I used motion graphics and fade in and out transitions to signify the transition from one subteam section to the next.

Third Video - 2019 Systems Acceptance Review

Introduction to Project

One year later, I was invited by one of the student leaders for the science subteam, Luke Piszkin, to take on the same Systems Acceptance Review video task for the next URC. At that time, I was the only one videographer around and the majority of my time with this project was spent coordinating with Luke on being available to help film during weekends when student members of subteams were available to work on some component of the rover. The constraints and workflow were very similar to those of last year for the previous SAR video, so the process of gathering and editing footage and voiceovers was more straightforward.

Footage Gathered

Yonder Dynamics had moved on to make a new rover for the year, but nevertheless the types of footage that I gathered during those weekends among other student members of Yonder Dynamics was not too different to the footage that I gathered for last year's SAR video. Most were based on the different student subteams that Yonder Dynamics had for each aspect of the 2019 rover, which were science, motions, electrical, and software. A couple of new CAD animations were gathered thanks to some of the student members who recorded them, and were used in the video (not shown below). Below are examples of footage that I have gathered.

Post-Production and Visual Effects

Luke provided me with a mellow soundtrack that was used as subtle background music, so the only post-production tasks that I did was taking logos used from a visual design person within Yonder Dynamics and using them with text graphics to showcase transitions from one subteam to the next. Transitions were kept to a minimal, as they were not really needed as much since it was best decided to let the voiceovers and footage showcase Yonder Dynamics' work in the video. However, I did include simple fade ins/outs and crossfades during the beginning and end of the video.

Below is the accompanying fundraising video for you view, which was published on Yonder’s Indiegogo campaign page.

Impact

Reflection

Admittedly, there were times when there were a lot of last minute deadlines that I had to navigate around while still being able to finish assembling all of the video clips together to create a cohesive, professional video for the competition judges. However, I was glad that there was a lot of creative freedom and direction with how to structure the video while still being able to work with project managers and student leaders on how to best tailor the videos for them and for the competition. Throughout the journey, I felt engaged with being able to handle voiceover sessions for video dialogue, getting footage of Yonder Dynamics' rover in action (it's not everyday you get to see a Mars rover moving around obstacles and even over a person), and improving on the visual design graphics of the video. It is a gratifying feeling when several weeks of honest, hard work and dedication to the video helps others get the funding they need to keep their rover building initiatives alive and to show off a super cool rover at a world competition.

That's concludes the media project! Thank you. I really do appreciate you taking a bit of your time reading what I have produced and learned. I only hope that you have learned something new from this project.
