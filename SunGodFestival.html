Overview

Project Type

These videos were both independently made video projects for ASCE.

Objective

To promote University of California - San Diego's biggest on-campus student events each year through video, Sun God Festival.

Duration

About 1 year with 9-10 iterations for the 2018 Trailer, 2 weeks for the 2019 Trailer

My Role

I managed all of the footage gathered, edited all media assets, privately shared the videos for feedback, and made changes to the videos based on feedback received.

Tools

Premiere Pro

Affiliations

Triton Television (TTV), Associated Students Concerts & Events (ASCE) at UC San Diego

Video Process for 2018 Trailer

Background

Sun God Festival (SGF) is a 6-hour artist festival for current and very recently graduated students. Historically, this festival remains a campus tradition and each year Associated Students Concerts & Events (ASCE) has spearheaded the task of managing all of the logistics of the event, including the inclusion of volunteers and invited visitors to help photograph and video record events that take place during the festival. For the 2018 Trailer, I was more strongly affiliated with Triton Television (TTV, a film and media production student organization on campus) as an ad-hoc editor who took the initiative to help craft a promotional trailer for the 2018 Sun God Festival to help ASCE market the event.

Project Beginnings

During Sun God Festival 2017 at the time, I was only part of TTV as a producer who helped other admin members and producers in TTV to gather media footage of that festival on behalf of ASCE. I expressed keen interest in solely taking on the role as the main editor for the next year's 2018 trailer, so I immersed myself, diving deep at the existing footage gathered from SGF 2017. I was not as experienced in video editing at the time, but I did my best to improve upon my abilities through subsequent drafts made.

Finding Inspiration

To better understand the theme and existing approaches to Sun God Festival, I was inspired to view past videos through YouTube and Vimeo of existing promotional videos dedicated for SGF. Although older videos promoted SGF as a more day-long event that extends into the night, the 2017 Trailer, in particular, seemed to best showcase the atmosphere that current students are experiencing. The warm yellow and orange tones of the people, objects, and social setting combined with the energetic dancing and smiles from students on camera left an impression that SGF was exciting, vibrant, social, carefree, and idyllic.

Finding a Soundtrack

To best reflect the narrative, tone, and intended mood of the SGF 2018 Trailer, I scoured online to find the best kind of music to choose from. The song I Want You (Feat. Matthew John Kurz) by Robotaki & Manila Killa had a gradual intro with an exciting buildup of tempo as well as an energetic music sequence towards the end, which seemed to be a great match for the trailer.

Video Editing

I wanted to test the waters of the footage and determine what sequences of shots would be appropriately placed in the video to capture what Sun God Festival should feel like. I assembled the first draft under the assumption that people either may or may not have experienced or known about this event before and I believed that showing clips of the diverse venues and experiences that the festival has to offer can position the video to have really rich, compelling, and persuasive storytelling for the event. Below were my main considerations for what sort of footage should be included in the 2018 trailer. These clips were derived from the first draft.

Event Activities and Experiences

I gathered clips of these activities recorded that help shape a true experience of being at Sun God Festival, such as taking photos, going in water slides, seeing art posters that paid tribute to the artists that came, and getting a henna.

People Who Attended

Of course, the people were just as important, since capturing people’s interactions with their surrounding environment of the festival is really evocative of how student-centered and social the festival embodied. Since the intended audience is mostly students, it was important to showcase others who have attended and who sincerely enjoyed the presence of the camera so that the experience of the festival seems relevant and persuasive to prospective students and viewers who aren't too sure about what the event is like.

Capturing the Concert Experience

Sun God Festival always has a large stage and plenty of artists To capture students’ needs of participating in the concert experience, I had to map out a portion of the video towards the end to capture artists performing and the crowds.

Evaluation and Feedback

After a few more earlier drafts made, I came to a clear understanding of some of the weaknesses that the 2018 Trailer had.

In general, the clips were way too short to be able to communicate what was going on, so it would be easy to be dissuaded by the rapid pacing of the video. It would be important to either give the clips breathing room or find replacing footage that is easier for a person to interpret in a split second.

From a color grading standpoint, the colors needed to be better balanced to more authentically showcase the festival. The visuals that were colored seem to make the festival a bit too flamboyant and unrealistically portrayed, with noticeably oversaturated spots on some of the clips. This can potentially led to an unpleasant experience for the viewer to be distracted by the mistakes made on the video, and leading the viewer's attention away from the content.

Within the earlier drafts, I initially assumed that visual effects can really help create a sense of flow with the video and a more consistent narrative. However, after getting feedback from members at Triton Television when I shared one of the drafts, I learned about the drawbacks of transitions. If overused and misused, light flares and blur transitions can really make the content unnecessarily blurred or obscured while making the content too overwhelming and confusing to understand. These types of transitions also reduce the time the viewer has in understanding the footage. It would make the transitions seem of greater importance, and not how they reinforce the content and narrative of the experience at SGF.

From the people at ASCE, it would be an appreciative gesture if I would be able to include logos or any footage that more easily signified a sponsor/product name (such as Rockstar or Doritos) or a food vendor.

Iterations

Besides including sponsor clips and replacing footage, below were the overall following changes and additions made to later drafts of the 2018 Trailer.

Extended Or Fewer Clips

This was relatively simple improvement to do. It involved removing clips that were unnessarily short and don't significantly contribute to the overall narrative of the trailer. Extending most of the remaining clips was just a few clicks away.

Improved Color Grading

I did a second attempt on color correcting and grading the footage, with a greater emphasis in more natural skin tones, natural grass colors, and pale blue skies. I also did my best to improve the consistency of the colors between every clip.

Quicker or Alternative Transitions

Since there were so many transitions throughout the video, I did my best to reduce the amount of necessary transitions and to make these transitions adhere to key changes to the soundtrack. Light flare transitions would sometimes be switched to quick blurs for less obtrusive flows from one clip to the next.

Changes to Graphics and Effects of Beginning and End of Trailer

As the nature of the drafts changed over time, so did my awareness to the branding of AS Concerts and Events (ASCE) relative to SGF. Since the footage was proprietary to Triton Television (TTV), it was necessary to indicate the collaboration between TTV and ASCE. The purpose of the video also changed over time, as the video wasn't always tailored as a promotional trailer for the upcoming Sun God Festival. Text graphics and effects from previous drafts have tailored the video as more of an event recap of SGF, but as the 2018 Trailer was approaching, the video meant more as a promotional trailer, which meant that I had to include additional details about the date of SGF 2018 and how to register for the event. The different visual effects of starting and ending the video had also changed over each subsequent draft as well as I started to achieve desired branding of the event.

Video Editing for 2019 Trailer

Background

As an official videographer intern for ASCE during the making of SGF 2019 Trailer, I noticed a lot of negativity surrounding the lack of enthusiasm students had with the logistics of SGF. It was understandable, given the increased budget that ASCE has solely for SGF, the unpleasantly long lines that occur, the underwhelming student reception to the artist lineups, and the expensive costs for hiring security crew.

Despite how disheartening and distressing it was to see this, it was a valuable opportunity to have people remember of how positive and timeless the festival was and to reminisce the moments that truly made the event special. The video would be a great medium for for making this kind of impression for people about the event.

Process

From gaining more hands-on experience with video editing projects, I was more familiar with the video editing process during this project. Past learnings from making the SGF 2018 Trailer were still fresh in mind to improve upon for the 2019 Trailer, so I quickly found relevant footage to highlight main activities, to focus on the people enjoying the event, and showcase a montage of artists that performed. The general structure was very similar to the 2018 video, in that this video also builds up the excitement, energy, and social interactions within the festival.

Finding and Editing the Soundtrack

A key soundtrack that helped to make this happen was Alive by Ikson. I experimented with the high-pass audio effect on Premiere Pro to only have high frequency tones audible to have the soundtrack have a more nostalgic tone to it. By switching the soundtrack back and forth between showing high pitched tones and showing the unedited version of the song across key moments of the video, an obvious distinction between more mellow moments versus more exciting moments of the soundtrack can be heard. This distinction, along with adding a record scratch effect, helped to develop an impression from the video of how timeless and nostalgic moments in SGF were.

Selecting Footage

Taking past learnings from making the 2018 Trailer, it was easier and more efficient this time around to identify and select footage that would highlight activities at the festival, people dancing and enjoying themselves in front of the camera, and showcasing the concert experience for students.

Coloring Footage

From the previous examples of video clips/reference images shown, there were obvious differences to the coloring. This was because I wanted to reflect the intended nostalgic tone of the video and to have people reminisce and look forward to the SGF experience. Moments of high pitched tones from the soundtrack would relate more to SGF activities and experiences in the past that are timeless. An old film look to the visuals with desaturated colors seemed appropriate for those high pitched tones of the soundtrack while a more contemporary look with more saturated colors and contrast would be used for the original, unedited portions of the soundtrack. Reflection

This project helped me to learn, apply, and improve upon my existing video post-production skills for upcoming projects. Despite the many small details that I overlooked, I felt that I quickly adapted and recalled these mistakes into the second Sun God Festival video. I'm glad that I also could showcase the student experience within this event that students at UC San Diego look forward to each year, and even impact social media engagement with the event. I learned about how research on typeface finding, image assets, free video stock footage, and past SGF videos really helped to solidify the visual design and storytelling of the videos. Most importantly, this experience with the videos gave me a greater appreciation and empathy of the viewers who want to understand what activities and experiences Sun God Festival had that promises a great concert festival for students, a lesson that I will always take with me to apply into future video projects.
