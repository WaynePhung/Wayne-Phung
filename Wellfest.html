Wellfest 2018 Recap

This was a recap of Wellfest, an event at University of California - San Diego campus geared towards awareness of zero waste, health, well-being, and sustainability.

Overview

Project Type

This was both a video redesign and a client project for Housing Dining and Hospitality (HDH).

Objective

To create a video that captures the different aspects of wellness within the Wellfest event.

My Role

I remixed a soundtrack, managed the footage, made drafts of the video, and made changes to each draft of the video before handing off the final version to HDH.

Tools

Premiere Pro

Core Team

This project was independently made by me, but I had a bit of help with feedback from Electra Martin and Shashi Mostafa on getting media assets and feedback on the drafts.

Affiliations

Triton Television

Background

This was a recap video for Wellfest, which was an event hosted on October 13th, 2018, hosted by the University of California - San Diego’s main campus organization, Housing Dining and Hospitality (HDH for short). HDH wanted a recap video made from Triton Television (TTV), a student film and media production organization that I was involved in. I was tasked by Electra Martin, one of the student executive members of TTV at the time, to fulfill HDH’s request for the video.

It was necessary for me to gain inspiration, context, and resources about how to effectively represent Wellfest within the recap video, and I did receive some help with this endeavor. With Electra, she was able to provide me with an initial edit of the Wellfest video made by Jack Mason-Brase, another member of Triton Television, as well as the original soundtrack that he made within that initial edit (also made by Jack).

There was another executive member in TTV who happened to also work with HDH, Shashi Mostafa. She gave me important media assets that I needed to include in the video, which consisted of the event text, silhouette logo, all of the sponsor logos that needed to be featured, and additional information of what the event was about.

Process

Electra told me that all of the video clips were pre-organized, so I retrieved the footage and started editing.

First Edit - Remixing a Soundtrack

I set a creative constraint to work and remix around the soundtrack, since the video's narrative would be revolved around it. The order of the soundtrack consisted of the following: a beginning portion would have a consistent tone to gradually help the viewer become immersed with the video, a faster tempo change would be followed, some rising action, another tempo change, more exuberant music to represent thefalling action, and an abrupt ending.

Gathering Footage

I quickly began to get confused along the way of how to properly structure the video clips since there was such a variety of clips available. However, I was already able to identify different categories that the clips had in common. They are shown below as a video montage of clips for each category.

I’ve also decided to play around with the visual design of the video a bit more. I used a color picker tool to extract colors from one of the Wellfest event graphics to make a giant picture that was an angled gradient of the colors. It would be used as the background for the end of the video when showing the sponsor logos.

This gradient was used as an appropriate background for showcasing the wellfest text, the silhouette logo, and the sponsor logos for the concluding sponsor credits that HDH has also requested to include. The resolution and quality of some of the sponsor logos given to me were quite variable, and some of them had colors that had too little contrast to the background, so I decided to make a subtle white background for some of them to improve legibility of the logos.

Rough Draft

Feedback

On my behalf, Electra sent this draft to HDH for feedback. From the beginning of the video draft, there was a sign that had a lot of text, which was competing for attention with the graphic text "presents", which may have confused Shashi when she reviewed it. So, a different clip was needed to replace the video clip of the sign.

Suggestions for Changes

Mona was a staff member of HDH and Shashi's boss, and she expressed many more suggestions for changes. She really wanted a greater divide between the different sections of the video, which meant that there should be more obvious and modular sets of clips that defined a key feature of the event, such as having a set of clips that showed more of the food and having another set of clips that showed more shots that emphasized zero waste.

More specifically and importantly, the different sections of the video should be able to capture the main aspects of wellness within the event, such as food, fitness, mental well-being, community/social experiences, environmentalism, etc.

There was also a need from her to add more text so that there is a clearer explanation on why the event exists, which was to promote wellness as part of the student experience.

Iterating

Brainstorming on Wellness

I dabbled with how to succinctly phrase the different dimensions of wellness that this event captured, but I realized that there was a distinction between what wellness was synonymous with and what wellness is meant for and that a single phrase with the words "wellness and" (topic) and "wellnesss with" (topic) seemed to succinctly outline the different aspects of the event as well as the event's purpose.

Different Ideas for Wellness Phrases

  • “Wellness is (insert word of dimension of wellness)”
  • “Wellness means…”
  • “Wellfest, a place for…”

Representing Wellness Graphically

I translated the phrasing of wellness with the visual design of the video by adding more text to the different clips that were important to highlight. To make the phrasing more apparent and the clips more relevant to each other, "wellness" would be the first word followed by text that had the appropriate association of wellness. Both parts of the text were angled and were shown with a subtle background with a color inspired by the event posters.

More Visual Effects

Other than different types of video transitions applied, I decided to play around with freezing video frames as a different segway into other featured themes of the event. I also added a fade in effect of the gradient background towards the end to smoothly transition from showing the wellfest text graphics into the sponsor credits.

Final Draft

Results and Reflection

The final video was handed off to HDH, which they accepted. However, this video was not published publicly as of late.

It was an interesting project about a redesign for a specific video that incorporated more of a video post-production process to it than a traditional double-diamond process that is generally accepted in the design world. I was stumped about how to best convey a story of an event recap without resorting to just a seemingly random montage of clips, but learning about the context of the seven dimensions of wellness and going back to the fundamentals of storytelling helped me to simplify the story of Wellfest while still making it a compelling recap to learn about. It was a reminder of how simplicity within storytelling helps to make sense of the story first so that other elements within post-production can contribute to make that story more engaging without confusing the narrative behind the story.
