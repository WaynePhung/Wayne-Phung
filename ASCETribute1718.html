ASCE 2017-18 Tribute

This was a tribute to most of the student campus events that happened within the 2017-18 academic year at University of California - San Diego. These events were hosted by the Associated Students Concerts and Events (ASCE).

Overview

Project Type

This was an independent video project for ASCE.

Objective

To showcase a comprehensive coverage of most of the main events featured from ASCE for the 2017-18 academic year at University of California - San Diego in a video.

Duration

1 week

My Role

I have stored all media assets obtained from other videographers and from ASCE events that I helped covered. I have managed the end-to-end process of editing the video, from footage consolidation to the finishing visual effects.

Tools

Photoshop, Premiere Pro

Affiliations

Associated Students Concerts & Events (ASCE) at UC San Diego

Event Coverage

I had lots of footage saved and organized throughout that particular academic year. So, I was able to quickly dive in and organize the different events such that footage from earlier events would show up earlier within the video and vice versa. The main challenge in assembling the footage together was to have a diverse set of clips that would give the viewer a sufficient idea about what each of the events were. Here was how I evaluated the clips, based on my initial understanding of each event and the clips that I analyzed.

Event Festivities

Festivals like Bear Garden, Bear Garden Senior Send Off, and Hullabaloo tend to have activities that best characterizes what they’re about. In the case of Bear Garden events, it was games like shooting through hoops, photo booths, and prizes. For Hullabaloo, it was carnival rides, and bright LED lights.

Concerts and Artists

Concerts have a stronger emphasis on artists and crowds. More specifically, it was important to capture artists performing onstage as well as the audience’s genuine enjoyment and excitement around to the artist’s song or music.

Guest Speakers

Similar to concerts with artists, events like Breaking Barriers have industry guest speakers featured in these events. Thus, it was important to show their engagement with the audience from different perspectives while also showing key clips that imply the types of topics that they discussed.

Graphics and Visual Effects

Extracting Event Text from Graphics

I did my best using Photoshop to edit out the text from some of the graphic posters that ASCE had or that some of the artists had, such that the text was only in white. This text would be shown at the beginning of each portion of the video from video footage of one event to the next and was done to easily identify and separate the events from one another.

Visual Effects

I experimented with adding a glowing, color-shifting background to the text and background clips and made it consistent across the beginning and end of the video. The content in the beginning acted as a hook to the viewer to fuel curiosity of what the video meant to be as a tribute to the year where as the content in the end simply summed up the video.

Results and Reflection

This video was uploaded on ASCE's YouTube channel as well as a public Facebook post after the end of the academic year.


This gives a comprehensive overview of what events ASCE is capable of. Although it could be shorter, I hoped that people who watch this get a good sense of how ASCE positively contributes to the student experience at UC San Diego and would inspire students to be more hopeful and excited about this small aspect of what the campus has to offer. It was not easy to manage multiple different folders of video clips that spanned multiple different events, but I'm glad that this video helped me realize what I could do to give back to others through my strengths in media production. I had lots of fun with going above and beyond to achieve new visual effects and leveraging them to craft a unique narrative of ASCE and how appreciative the organization is to students.
